AUM SRI SAI RAM

The difference between Procedural & OO-programming:

  • Procedural programming is a brute force kind of implementation of ‘what’ one wants to do.
  • OO-programming concentrates on design of the program than a brute force method to solve problems.

The difference between these can be likened to that of the difference between Bhakthi & Karma margas. The Bhakthi (in its pure form, unlike that of Hanuman who had intelligence flavouring his devotion) is just a blind but guaranteed path to God, just as the procedural language (can be intelligently procedural much akin to Hanuman’s devotion) while OO-programming is like the Gnana marga, concentrating on the path as much as the goal.

The difference between the algorithm & the program itself is similar to the difference between preaching & practice. The value of the algorithm is not realized until it is implemented as a program. In a similar way, there is no use of philosophy without practice!

The main() function is something that is common to most HLLs(High Level Languages). Its importance lies in the fact that it is the starting point of the execution of any program. Without the main(), the program cannot run! It is much akin to Human Effort ( Purusha Prayatna) even if man knows everything, he cannot achieve any thing in his life if he does not put efforts in that direction. While writing the programs, if everything else is coded but not the main(), it still is useless because, there has been no effort put in that direction.

The assert() macro that is provided with the preprocessor for many compilers is a phenomenon thatcan be compared to the Discrimination power of the Mind. This is because the assert() macro asserts the ‘goodness’ of an expression to be evaluated. In a similar way, we must always assert() our actions before ‘executing’ them!The before is obvious because the assert() is a macro that is defined for the pre-processor. So,our action also must be pre-processed or in other words, we must Discriminate before we act.

The programming ‘idioms’ like while(true) or for(;;) are like the world. It is an infinite cycle of the same birth & death cycle; if we get caught in it, we are done for. We must device very hard-core brute-force methods like the ctrl+D interrupts to jerk out of it. But, we should note that these idioms are essential & hence not flagged as errors by the compilers. So also, the world is essential; it is God’s creation. He has created it with a purpose. Be in the world; not of it! So, if we happen to get caught in the infinite loop called the world, we must have mechanisms called prayers to jerk ourselves out of it. When we pray, God executes a ctrl+D to get us back to reality!

In OO-languages, the one of the corner stones is Inheritance. It is a mechanism that is used to implement extensibility & object-code reuse. But, care has been taken to prevent (by default), inherit the private part of the class. Indeed, it is said: “Speak less than thou knowest; show less than thou havest!” The OO-languages follow the pattern by implementing this: private data is not ‘shown’ while the private functions are not ‘spoken’ in this mechanism called inheritance.

The set up of the computer is much like a human body:

CPU

Head

It does the processing

Monitor

Face

It reflects the head

RAM

Heart

It should, ideally, forget the bad other s have done to it & the good it has done to others! And RAM is volatile ( it ‘forgets’ everything when shut down!)

Keyboard & Mouse

Sense Organs

Obvious?

UPS

Food

No food => no energy => no functionality

OS

Life / Soul

With out the OS, the computer is lifeless!

From this analogy, just as we take care of the Computer & the Hard-disk against dust, viruses etc. we must also keep the body &amp; mind clean; free from evil thoughts & tendencies. This is very important.

The Compiler is the key component in the life of any computer. It is the compiler that judges the actions you intend to do ( it parses & checks the code for syntax & possibly certain semantic errors , depending on the intelligence of the compiler ).Once the program is compiled, it is clear that the programmer has to take responsibility for his actions(the compiler does not check if you write ‘malicious’ code or even bad code; you are responsible for that & also the results that follow). Doesn’t this sound like God’s reign? He lets man do what he wants, but man is responsible for the results of his actions.

We can even bring in the Karma theory. We take rebirth ( as per the theory ) to atone for our sins & make progress towards perfection. In a similar way, we keep recompiling the program & running it till we have no messages like ‘This program has performed an illegal operation & will be closed by Windows’ or ‘Segmentation Fault’ or ‘Aborted’. We keep compiling & running these programs till they work right. The compiler is like the ‘Eternal witness’, simply dispensing the results as per the actions. Same with God & man. But, the analogy is not very good. Because, God is perfect; compilers are not. Many compilers have bugs & vary in their intelligence. But God is one & He is perfect. Compilers can be thought of as Messiahs or apostles. They guide man in his quest for ‘perfection’ & no more.

There are many more such simple parallels between real life & the way computers live!
